Sentiment Analysis

You'll Master: Reading market mood, using sentiment indicators, and spotting crowd psychology.

Why Sentiment Analysis?

Sentiment analysis helps you gauge the overall mood of the market—whether traders are feeling bullish, bearish, or uncertain. Understanding sentiment can help you anticipate reversals and avoid crowd traps.

1. Sentiment Indicators

Tools to Measure the Crowd:

Sentiment indicators show how traders are positioned. They include tools like the Commitment of Traders (COT) report, retail positioning, and sentiment indexes.

Key Actions

  • Monitor COT reports for institutional positioning
  • Check retail sentiment from brokers
  • Watch for extreme readings as reversal signals

2. Crowd Psychology

Understand the Herd:

Markets often move on emotion—fear and greed. Recognizing crowd behavior helps you avoid emotional trades and spot opportunities when the majority is wrong.

Examples

  • Sharp rallies after panic selling
  • Reversals after euphoric buying
  • Contrarian trades when sentiment is extreme

3. Combining Sentiment & Other Analysis

Layer Your Edge:

Use sentiment as a filter—combine it with technical and fundamental analysis for higher probability trades. Sentiment can warn you when a trend is overextended.

  • Check sentiment before entering trades
  • Be cautious when sentiment is at extremes
  • Use sentiment to confirm or question your setups

4. Sentiment Analysis Checklist

  • ☑️ Review sentiment indicators regularly
  • ☑️ Identify extreme bullish or bearish readings
  • ☑️ Watch for divergence between price and sentiment
  • ☑️ Combine with technical and fundamental analysis
  • ☑️ Stay objective—avoid following the crowd blindly

Practice Zone

Homework:

  • Find and bookmark a live Forex sentiment tool
  • Track sentiment for a currency pair and note price reactions
  • Write a short note on how sentiment affected a recent trade

Next Step: Let sentiment guide your awareness—trade with the crowd, not against it!
