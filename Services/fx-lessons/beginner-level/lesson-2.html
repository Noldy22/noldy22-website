Lesson 2: Market Basics

Objective: Master currency pair fundamentals, price calculations, and market terminology

Currency Pairs

Definition:

A currency pair represents the quotation of two different currencies, where the value of one currency (base) is quoted against the other (quote currency). Example: EUR/USD = 1.0850 means 1 Euro buys 1.0850 US Dollars.

Detailed Breakdown:

Major Pairs (7)

  • EUR/USD (Euro/Dollar)
  • USD/JPY (Dollar/Yen)
  • GBP/USD (Pound/Dollar)
  • USD/CHF (Dollar/Swiss Franc)
  • USD/CAD (Dollar/Canadian Dollar)
  • AUD/USD (Australian Dollar/Dollar)
  • NZD/USD (New Zealand Dollar/Dollar)

Why They Matter:
• 80% liquidity concentration
• Average spread: 0.8-1.5 pips
• High news sensitivity

Cross Pairs

Pairs without USD but still significant:

Pair Nickname Average Daily Range
EUR/GBP Euro-Pound 60 pips
AUD/JPY Aussie-Yen 80 pips

Note: Higher spreads (2-5 pips) due to lower liquidity

Pips & Lots

Advanced Pip Understanding:

1 pip = 0.0001 for most pairs, except JPY pairs where 1 pip = 0.01
Example: EUR/USD moves from 1.0850 to 1.0851 = 1 pip movement
USD/JPY moves from 150.00 to 150.01 = 1 pip movement

Pip Value Formula:

Pip Value = (1 pip / Exchange Rate) × Lot Size

USD/CHF Example:
0.0001 / 0.9100 × 100,000 = $10.99 per pip

Bid/Ask Dynamics

Bid Price Mechanics

The maximum price buyers will pay for currency:
Market Maker Perspective: The price at which they'll buy from you

If Bid = 1.0850, selling EUR returns $1.0850 per €1

Ask Price Mechanics

The minimum price sellers will accept:
Market Maker Perspective: The price at which they'll sell to you

If Ask = 1.0852, buying EUR costs $1.0852 per €1

Spread Analysis

What Affects Spreads?

  • Liquidity: Major pairs < Exotic pairs
  • Market Hours: Spreads widen during off-hours
  • Volatility: News events increase spreads

Live Market Example

EUR/USD
1.0850 +2 1.0852

Cost Analysis: Opening and closing 1 standard lot position costs $20 (2 pips × $10/pip)

Applied Learning

1. Calculate pip value for 2 mini lots of GBP/USD at 1.2700:

Hint: (0.0001/1.2700) × 20,000 units

2. During NFP announcement:
- Previous spread: 1.2 pips
- Current spread: 5.8 pips
How much more does it cost to trade 3 standard lots?

Extra Cost = (5.8 - 1.2) × $10 × 3 = $138

Market Maker Analogy

"Imagine a currency exchange booth at an airport:
• Bid Price = What they pay for your foreign currency
• Ask Price = What they charge for local currency
• Spread = Their profit margin
The busier the airport (market volatility), the wider their spread!"
