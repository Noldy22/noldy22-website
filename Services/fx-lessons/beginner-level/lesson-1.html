Lesson 1: Introduction to Forex Trading

Objective: Understand Forex fundamentals, market structure, and key participants

What is Forex?

Official Definition:

Forex (Foreign Exchange) is the global decentralized market for trading national currencies.

Core Concepts:

Currency Exchange

Conversion of one currency to another at agreed rates

Example: Japanese tourist exchanging JPY for EUR in Paris

Market Structure

  • OTC (Over-the-Counter) Market
  • No physical central exchange
  • 24/5 operational hours

Three Main Purposes:

International Trade

Companies convert currencies for cross-border transactions

Case Study: Apple paying Chinese suppliers in CNY

Speculation

Profiting from currency value fluctuations

80% of daily volume is speculative

Hedging

Reducing foreign exchange risk

Example: Airbus hedging USD/EUR exposure

Why Trade Forex?

$6.6 Trillion

Daily trading volume (Bank for International Settlements 2023)

170+ Currencies

Traded globally

Liquidity Advantage

EUR/USD average daily volume: $1.2 trillion

Accessibility

  • Micro lots ($1,000 positions)
  • Leverage up to 100:1
  • $100 minimum accounts

Leverage Warning

100:1 leverage means $100 controls $10,000 position

5% price move = 500% profit/loss
